What is CBAM?

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism turns import data into a compliance workflow.

CBAM requires EU importers in carbon-intensive sectors to track embedded emissions, gather supplier evidence, and submit quarterly transitional reports. The hard part is not knowing the rule exists; it is keeping supplier data, product codes, emissions factors, and report deadlines aligned.

Frequently asked

CBAM includes a 50-tonne annual threshold per importer. If your total CBAM-scope imports across all regulated goods (steel, cement, aluminium, fertilizers, hydrogen, electricity) are below 50 tonnes per year, you are currently exempt from the transitional reporting obligation. However, this threshold applies to the total, not per product — and it may be revised as CBAM moves from the transitional to the definitive phase in 2026. We recommend tracking your volumes now to avoid last-minute compliance gaps.
The six product categories currently in scope under Regulation (EU) 2023/956 are: Steel (iron and steel), Cement, Aluminium, Fertilizers, Hydrogen, and Electricity. These cover specific CN codes listed in Annex I of the regulation. Products outside these categories — such as plastics, glass, or paper — are not subject to CBAM at this stage, though the European Commission has signalled potential expansion beyond 2030.

For the definitive phase, importers must use actual embedded emissions verified by an accredited verifier — our default factors are a conservative proxy for planning purposes.
